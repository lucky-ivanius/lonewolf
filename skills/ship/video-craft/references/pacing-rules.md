# Pacing rules

Frame-level rules. Pacing problems are the most common reason a video "feels off" without an obvious cause.

## Dead frames

- Definition: 0.5 seconds or longer with no visual change and no narrative reason for the pause.
- Fix: trim the dead frame, or replace with a smash cut to the next beat.
- Exception: a held shot that lets the viewer absorb a key moment (e.g. a transaction confirmed UI). These are intentional, not dead.

## Rushed cuts

- Definition: a cut under 0.5 seconds where the viewer cannot read the on-screen text or process the visual.
- Fix: extend by 0.5 to 1.5 seconds, or move the on-screen text to a slower beat.

## Cut rhythm

- Faster cuts heighten energy; slower cuts deepen attention. Match the cut rhythm to the beat:
 - Hook: fast (0.5 to 1 second per cut).
 - Demo: medium (1 to 2 seconds per cut, or one held shot showing a flow).
 - CTA: slow (hold the frame for the viewer to read and act).

## On-screen text

- 3 to 5 words per line.
- 1.5 to 2 seconds on screen per line.
- Burned in for muted plays.
- Lower third for landscape, center for vertical.

## Motion

- Avoid camera movement during on-screen text. The viewer cannot read while the frame moves.
- Limit zooms and pans to short bursts (0.5 to 1 second) used as transitions, not as a constant motion.

## Demo cuts

- Cut to the success state of the user flow, not the menu.
- Show input visibly: a click, a typed value, a wallet approval. The viewer needs to see action to internalize the demo.
- If the load-bearing integration is not shown clearly in the demo cut, it is probably not yet load-bearing.

## Common mistakes

- Logo splash before the hook.
- Long establishing shot that does not show the product.
- Slow camera pan that uses 3 seconds for a beat that should be 1.
- Talking head intro before the demo. The viewer wants the demo first.
- Multiple cut directions stacked (zoom + pan + dissolve). Pick one transition shape and use it consistently.
