# Script beats

Templates for 30, 45, and 60 second marketing videos. Cut beats to compress; never extend beats to fill.

## 30 seconds

- 0:00 to 0:03: hook. One sentence, on-screen text.
- 0:03 to 0:08: problem. One sentence over a quick visual.
- 0:08 to 0:18: solution demo. The load-bearing flow at success state.
- 0:18 to 0:25: load-bearing sponsor moment, if applicable. Show the integration in action.
- 0:25 to 0:30: CTA. One ask, one URL.

## 45 seconds

- 0:00 to 0:03: hook.
- 0:03 to 0:10: problem with a concrete example.
- 0:10 to 0:25: solution demo, end to end.
- 0:25 to 0:35: sponsor moment plus one wow detail.
- 0:35 to 0:40: social proof if any (real numbers from `business-model.md` or `will-pay.md`, cited). Skip if none.
- 0:40 to 0:45: CTA.

## 60 seconds

- 0:00 to 0:03: hook.
- 0:03 to 0:12: problem and the painful current behavior.
- 0:12 to 0:30: solution demo, the full load-bearing flow.
- 0:30 to 0:42: sponsor moment, with the user-visible benefit named.
- 0:42 to 0:50: social proof or a unique product detail.
- 0:50 to 0:55: brief team or "made by" beat, optional.
- 0:55 to 1:00: CTA.

## Beat-level rules

- One sentence per beat in the voiceover. Two clauses max.
- One on-screen text overlay per beat. The overlay extends, never duplicates, the voiceover.
- Demo cuts at the success state of the user flow. No empty UI; no settings page.
- If the load-bearing integration cannot be shown convincingly in 8 to 12 seconds, it is probably not yet load-bearing. Fix the product, not the video.

## Bad beats to avoid

- "We are excited to introduce..." Cut. The hook is the introduction.
- A logo splash before the hook. The viewer is gone before the logo finishes.
- A demo that pans through every menu before showing value. Lead with value.
- A multi-CTA close ("follow, sign up, join Discord, buy"). Pick one.
