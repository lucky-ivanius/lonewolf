# Channel specs

Per-channel rules for length, aspect ratio, on-screen text, and audio behavior. Default to the most restrictive when planning a multi-channel cut.

## Twitter / X video

- Length: 30 to 45 seconds for organic reach. Up to 60 if the content earns it.
- Aspect ratio: 16:9 for native, 9:16 for vertical native, 1:1 acceptable for in-feed.
- Plays: muted by default. On-screen text must carry the message.
- Caption: text post under 240 characters. Lead with the claim.

## YouTube Shorts

- Length: 15 to 60 seconds. Sweet spot 30 to 45.
- Aspect ratio: 9:16, full vertical.
- Plays: with audio by default.
- Hook in the first 3 seconds. Loops are a bonus; design the last frame to flow into the first.

## Landing page (embedded)

- Length: 30 to 60 seconds. Could go longer if the page is content-heavy and the video is a deeper feature.
- Aspect ratio: 16:9 standard, 1:1 if the page layout demands it.
- Plays: muted autoplay typically; user clicks to unmute.
- Pair with a static fallback poster frame for users who do not play.

## Multi-channel cut

When the same video is targeted at multiple channels, plan once and crop:

- Master at 16:9, with the focal action centered for safe 9:16 and 1:1 crops.
- On-screen text positioned in the safe area for all crops.
- Captions burned in (not just SRT) so muted plays still carry the message.

## On-screen text rules

- Font: high-contrast, legible at small size.
- Position: lower third for landscape, center for square / vertical so it survives interface chrome.
- Duration on screen: 1.5 to 2 seconds per line. Three to five words per line.
- Color: brand color if accessible, otherwise white with a soft drop shadow.

## Audio rules

- Voiceover: only if the project genuinely benefits. A muted-friendly cut with on-screen text often outperforms a voiceover.
- Music: license clearly. Do not assume a track is free because it is in a sample library.
- SFX: a single product sound (transaction confirmed, success ping) is more memorable than a stack of stock sfx.
