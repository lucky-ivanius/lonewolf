# Severity rubric

Rank weaknesses by blast radius, not by how it feels to the user.

## High severity

These are the weaknesses that, if a judge or investor sees them in the first 30 seconds, kill the conversation.

- Generic positioning. The brain pattern-matches to "yet another X" and stops engaging.
- Unclear value. The viewer cannot describe what the product does after watching the demo.
- Demo theater. The reviewer notices a hidden failure case and loses trust.
- Tech that is not load-bearing. The reviewer concludes the chain claim is marketing.

Fix all high-severity items before submission. They compound: a generic position with hidden demo failures is twice as bad as either alone.

## Medium severity

These cost the project momentum but do not kill the conversation outright.

- Missing competitive moat (the reviewer asks "why can't I do this?" and the user does not have a clean answer).
- Pricing that does not make sense (the reviewer can spot the unit economics gap).
- Brand that is forgettable (the reviewer cannot recall the project two days later).

Fix at least one before submission. All three is ideal.

## Low severity

Recoverable later, not the highest priority before submission.

- Minor copy errors.
- Inconsistent typography on internal pages.
- A few rough loading states that do not actually break the demo.

Note them in the roast but do not put them in the top 3.

## How to pick the top 3

Default rule: pick the three highest-severity items the user can fix in 24 hours.

If there are more than three high-severity items, the project may not be ready to submit. Recommend the user defer the submission window or cut scope to a smaller, sharper claim.

If there are fewer than three high-severity items, fill the remaining slots with the highest-severity medium items.

## What top 3 is NOT

- Not the three easiest items to fix. Easy items are not necessarily impactful.
- Not the items the user asked you to focus on. The user does not always see their own blind spots; that is why they asked for a roast.
- Not the items where you have the most clever critique. Severity, not wit.
