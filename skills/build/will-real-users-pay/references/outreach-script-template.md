# Outreach script templates

Short, direct, concrete. The price is in the script. Hiding the price invalidates the test.

## DM template

> Hi <name>, I'm building <one-sentence problem statement>. The product <one-sentence offer> for <price> per <month | transaction | report>. You came up because you mentioned <specific recent post or comment about the problem>. Would you pay this? Honest answer is fine, including no.

Rules:

- Name the candidate by name.
- Cite the specific reason you reached out (their post, their work, their public profile). Generic outreach is noise.
- Lead with the problem, not the product.
- The price and the cadence are explicit.
- Ask a yes / no question.
- Invite a no.

Length: 3 to 5 sentences. If it does not fit on a phone screen, it is too long.

## Landing page copy template

```
<one line headline naming the problem>
<one paragraph, 2 to 3 sentences, naming the offer and the price>
<one CTA button: "Reserve at <price>" or "Join waitlist">
<one line below the CTA: who this is for, who it is not for>
```

Rules:

- The price is on the page above the fold.
- The CTA names the price.
- "Who it is not for" filters out non-candidates and improves signal quality.
- No hero illustrations, no testimonials from people who have not paid. The page is a test, not marketing.

## Concierge offer template

> I'm validating a paid version of <product>. For <price>, I will <specific manual outcome> for you in the next <X> days. If you don't get value, full refund, no questions. Want it?

Rules:

- One specific outcome, one timeline, one price.
- Refund commitment in writing.
- Send to 5 candidates. If 1 to 2 say yes at this price, the signal is strong.

## Common mistakes

- Pitching the technology stack instead of the outcome. The candidate does not care that it runs.
- Bundling features into the offer. Test one offer at one price.
- Asking "would you use this?" instead of "would you pay $X for this?". The first is free; the second is the test.
- Following up to "convince" the candidate. The first answer is the signal. Do not relitigate.
