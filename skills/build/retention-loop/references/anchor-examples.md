# Anchor examples

Real loops from successful products, by anchor type. Use as a reference for what concrete answers look like.

## Day 1 anchors

The day-1 anchor is the moment the user gets value from the first session.

- **Trade execution**: a trader places their first trade and sees it settle in seconds (trading products).
- **First publish**: a creator publishes their first piece and gets a shareable, provable link (creator products).
- **First proof**: an identity-proofed user generates their first verifiable credential (identity products).
- **First save**: a user backs up their first document to durable storage with verifiable proof (archive products).
- **First yield**: a depositor sees their first interest accrual (lending and fintech products).

A vague day 1 ("they explore the app") is not an anchor. The user must DO something specific.

## Day 2 anchors

The day-2 anchor is the trigger that brings them back tomorrow.

- **State change**: an order filled or partially filled overnight, requiring the user to check.
- **Counterparty action**: another user replied, mentioned, or interacted with their content.
- **Threshold event**: a price hit a level, a position approached liquidation, a milestone unlocked.
- **Daily question**: the product asks the user a specific question that is fresh today (a poll, a market signal, a research prompt).
- **Maintenance need**: a position requires rebalancing, a subscription needs renewal, a lock period unlocked.

If the day-2 anchor is "they remember to check", the loop is broken. The product has to be the one that triggers the check.

## Day 7 anchors

The day-7 anchor is the moment of week-1 commitment.

- **Compounded outcome**: yields visible, position grew or shrank in a meaningful way.
- **History review**: a weekly digest of trades, mints, or saves the user can scroll through.
- **Goal review**: a goal the user set on day 1 is now testable (savings target, trading goal, learning goal).
- **Community event**: weekly meetup, weekly content drop, weekly governance vote.

If day 7 is just "they keep using the app", there is no specific moment. Force the user to name the moment.

## Day 30 anchors

The day-30 anchor is the moment of monthly identity.

- **Statement**: a monthly statement, performance summary, or review.
- **Subscription renewal or upgrade decision**: forced choice point.
- **Reputation increment**: a tier, badge, or status that compounds monthly.
- **Long-form review**: monthly retrospective the user expects.

If day 30 looks identical to day 1 (no compounding state, no unlocked features, no relationship deepening), the product has no monthly loop.

## Cross-anchor laddering

Strong loops ladder. Day 1 sets up day 2. Day 2 plus day 7 compound. Day 30 is the monthly anchor that earns commitment.

Bad loops have anchors that are independent. Each anchor stands alone, no compounding.

When reviewing the user's anchors, check: does day 1 invest the user in day 2? Does day 7 reward the day-2 commitment? Does day 30 honor the week-of-using?

## Cadence types

Not every product is daily. Some products are weekly, monthly, or event-driven. The skill accepts cadence variation as long as the user is honest about it:

- **Daily**: most consumer apps, social, trading bots.
- **Weekly**: governance products, weekly digests, weekly learning.
- **Monthly**: subscription services, statements, billing.
- **Event-driven**: products that trigger only on external events (e.g. only when the user has a real transaction need).

If the product is event-driven, the day-1/2/7/30 anchors translate to "first event / second event / fifth event / tenth event". The shape is the same; the time scale differs.

## Use during the workflow

When the user struggles to articulate an anchor, point them at this file. Often a concrete example unblocks them.
